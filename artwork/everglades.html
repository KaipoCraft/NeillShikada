The Headwaters

A pair of towers, a pair of people, a pair of birds, enjoying the Floridian winter. Shingle Creek is the beginning of the Everglades watershed just south of Orlando. Hidden confusingly behind a middle school, the trailhead brings you into a luscious green swamp, dense with cypress trees and knotted knees. It suddenly opens up to reveal a monolithic hallway of power lines that give you a third-person view of the swamp on either side - able to witness the largest abundance of birds I have ever seen.
